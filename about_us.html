We take our inspiration from other successful initiatives such as


Robin Hood Army

Globally, one in nine people in the world today (815 million) are undernourished

The Robin Hood Army is a volunteer-based Zero funds organization that works to get surplus food from restaurants to the less fortunate sections of society in cities across India and 14 other countries. The organization consists of over 78,262 volunteers (approximately) in 204 cities, and have served food to over 66.17 million people so far. The volunteers people from all walks of life. The less fortunate sections who the organization reaches out to include homeless families, orphanages, night shelters, homes for abandoned children, patients from public hospitals, etc.




Delhi Foodbanking Network

One in four of the world’s children suffer stunted growth. In developing countries, the proportion can rise to one in three.

This is aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) 2 to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition. We work to end hunger and work to ensure all vulnerable children have access safe, nutritious and sufficient food every day with education. We work to end all forms of malnutrition to reduce stunting and wasting of children.Through the years, we have fed over 20 million meals to over 17,000 children in need, starting from one feeding center in Delhi to over 70 centers across 12 States in India. These centers are mainly non-formal educational setups where we connect freshly cooked meals to children every day with the objective of bettering their health, education and safety. Our work revolves around achieving Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) of Zero Hunger that can positively impact our present and future generations.
